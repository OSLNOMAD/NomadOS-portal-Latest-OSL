## Customer Portal Cancellation & Retention Flow (Proposed Design)

### Context

We already have a customer portal in place for Nomad Internet. What we need now is a **structured, guided cancellation flow** inside the portal that focuses on **clarity, retention, and proper escalation**, instead of letting customers cancel blindly or flood support.

---

## 1. Subscription Overview Page

On the **Subscription page**, clearly show:

* All subscriptions associated with the customer
* Mark the **current/active subscription**
* Display subscription status clearly:

  * Active
  * Paused
  * Trial

For any subscription that is **Active, Paused, or Trial**, show a **“Cancel Subscription”** button.

---

## 2. Cancel Subscription Entry Point

When the customer clicks **Cancel Subscription**, do **not** cancel immediately.

Instead, present a **“Why are you cancelling?”** step with predefined reasons (radio buttons or dropdown).
Examples:

* Too expensive
* Slow speeds / performance issues
* Internet not reliable
* No longer needed
* Moving / changing provider
* Other (free-text)

The customer **must select one reason** to proceed.

---

## 3. Reason-Based Intelligent Flows

### A. Reason: “Too Expensive”

1. System checks the customer’s current subscription price (e.g. $99.95).
2. Show a retention offer:

   * Example: “We may be able to reduce your monthly cost.”
3. Ask:

   * “What price would work better for you?” (input field)

If the customer enters a target price:

* Evaluate it against predefined discount rules.
* Present a counter-offer:

  * Example:

    * 20% off next month
    * $20 off for the next 1–2 months

If customer accepts:

* Apply discount
* Keep subscription active
* End flow successfully

If customer rejects:

* Move to secondary retention offer
* If still rejected, continue cancellation flow

---

### B. Reason: “Slow Speeds / Internet Issues”

1. Show a dialog asking for clarification:

   * “Please describe the speed or connection issues you’re experiencing.”
2. Offer troubleshooting before cancellation:

   * “Would you like to try our guided troubleshooting flow?”

If customer selects **Yes**:

* Redirect to a **dedicated troubleshooting flow**
* Based on outcome:

  * If issue resolved → keep subscription
  * If unresolved → continue cancellation flow

If customer selects **No**:

* Proceed directly to retention offers or escalation

Optional retention:

* Offer goodwill credit or discount (example: 20% off next month)

---

### C. Other Cancellation Reasons

For other reasons:

* Capture short explanation (optional text field)
* Optionally show light retention offers based on reason
* Proceed forward without blocking

---

## 4. Final Escalation & Human Follow-Up

If the customer completes all steps and still wants to cancel:

Ask for **preferred follow-up method**:

* Email
* Phone call

If **phone call**:

* Ask for:

  * Preferred phone number
  * Best time to call

---

## 5. Internal Ticket & Automation

Once the flow completes:

### Zendesk

* Create a ticket in the **Cancellation / Retention group**
* Include:

  * Subscription ID
  * Customer ID
  * Cancellation reason
  * All responses provided by the customer
  * Preferred contact method
* Add all captured data as **internal notes**

### Slack Notification

* Trigger a POST request to a backend endpoint
* Send a Slack message including:

  * Customer name
  * Email
  * Subscription ID
  * Cancellation reason
  * Zendesk ticket ID
  * Current subscription status

---

## 6. Backend Endpoint

* Single endpoint to receive the cancellation flow payload
* Payload includes:

  * Customer details
  * Subscription details
  * Selected reason
  * Retention offers shown / accepted / rejected
  * Zendesk ticket ID

This endpoint:

* Posts to Slack
* Confirms ticket creation
* Logs cancellation intent for analytics

---

## Outcome

This flow:

* Prevents impulsive cancellations
* Introduces structured retention logic
* Reduces manual support workload
* Creates clean internal visibility (Zendesk + Slack)
* Gives customers control without chaos

This is the right way to do it.
Anything less than this is just a “Cancel” button pretending to be a system.
